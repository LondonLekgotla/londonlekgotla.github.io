Who We Are

Born from a need for
visibility and belonging

London Lekgotla was founded by a South African banker who recognised a pattern: Southern Africans in the UK often arrive without the family networks or community ties that other diasporas build on. We were here — in finance, law, medicine, tech — but rarely in the same room.

What began as a small circle of professionals has grown into a network spanning industries and life stages. We focus on the Southern African diaspora not out of exclusion, but intention: to build relationships we can lean on, and to channel our collective energy toward the communities and countries that shaped us.

We are proudly Southern African, proudly Pan-African — and committed to giving our community the voice and visibility it deserves.

This is where excellence meets heritage.
This is London Lekgotla.
What We Do

Three pillars,
one community

Looking to find your people, grow your impact, and reconnect with your roots? There is a place for you here.

01
🤝

Community & Connection

We create space for diaspora professionals to connect authentically across industries, generations, and life stages. From rooftop mixers and cultural watch parties to wine nights and welcome dinners — our events are where shared identity meets shared joy.

  • Cultural events & networking socials
  • WhatsApp & digital community groups
  • Newcomer welcome initiatives
02
🌱

Mentorship & Professional Growth

We believe in paying it forward. Our buddy and mentorship programmes match new arrivals and early-career professionals with established members. We host business dialogues, leadership panels, and invite guest speakers from across the UK and Africa.

  • Lekgotla Buddy Programme
  • Skills workshops & speaker events
  • Career navigation & peer coaching
03
🌍

Purpose & Impact

We are more than our LinkedIn profiles. London Lekgotla is a platform to uplift our communities back home and in the UK — through fundraising, diaspora philanthropy, and partnerships with grassroots initiatives that reflect who we are and where we come from.

  • Community giving & charity drives
  • Diaspora-led fundraising efforts
  • Advocacy for Southern African causes

New Joiners

Everything you need to
hit the ground running

Moving to the UK as a Southern African professional is exhilarating — and complicated. Visas, tax obligations in two countries, NHS dental surprises, ILR absence tracking, the Life in the UK test, professional re-registration. It adds up.

Our New Joiners Handbook is a community-built, regularly updated guide covering everything from day one to citizenship — written by people who have been through it.
